## A23- Enhance the cybersecurity at your home.


For this activity, I tried to improve the cybersecurity of my home in a practical way instead of treating it like a large technical project. One of the first things I did was log in to the router/admin page and review the devices connected to the home network. This helped me check whether all the visible devices actually belonged to people in the house and whether there were any unfamiliar devices connected. I also reviewed the router settings more carefully and thought about what extra protection could be added from there. One useful improvement is enabling a guest network, because that would let visitors use the internet without connecting directly to the main home network where our personal devices are connected. I think this is a strong extra security layer because it separates guest usage from the private network and reduces unnecessary access to the devices we use every day. So instead of giving visitors the main Wi-Fi, the guest network can act as a safer separate space for temporary access.

Another area I focused on was the laptop and browser environment, because that is where many daily risks actually happen. I reviewed the browser extensions installed on my laptop and removed the ones that were unnecessary or no longer used, since some extensions can have broad access to browsing activity. I also checked the browser’s security settings and made sure safer options such as HTTPS-first/secure connection settings were enabled, so that browsing would prefer secure versions of websites whenever possible. Along with that, I checked the system’s built-in protections such as the firewall and made sure they were turned on. These were small changes, but together they made the home digital environment feel more controlled and better protected.

I also thought about home security in a wider way, not only in terms of devices and internet settings. One issue I noticed was that the back side of the house is very dark at night. Even though the neighbour’s CCTV already overlooks that area, I decided to add a light around the house compound so that the space is properly visible at night and clearer on the camera as well. I felt this was an important improvement because security is stronger when the physical and digital sides support each other. Overall, this activity helped me understand that improving cybersecurity at home is often about making several small but sensible changes, both in the network itself and in the environment around it.
