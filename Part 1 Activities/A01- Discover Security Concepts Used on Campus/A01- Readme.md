## A01 – Discover security concepts used on campus

Security on campus at UWA can be seen as a layered system that combines surveillance, controlled access, emergency support, and human monitoring. One clear example is Reid Library, one of the most heavily used study spaces on campus. UWA states that currently enrolled students and staff have 24-hour access to the ground and first floors of Reid Library via Campus Card, which means access is restricted by identity after standard public hours. This is a strong security concept because it shows access control in practice: the building is open more broadly at some times, but after hours entry is limited to authorised users only. Another visible concept is security patrols and monitoring. While studying in Reid Library, it is common to see security staff moving through floors and checking the space for suspicious activity or safety issues. This adds a human layer of protection that supports deterrence and fast response, rather than relying only on gates or cameras. Across the wider campus, UWA also operates security services such as patrols, alarms, emergency response, and safety support, which shows that campus security is built around multiple controls working together instead of one single measure.

Another important campus security concept is digital identity protection. UWA’s student systems use UniID as the university’s access management system, which students use with their student number and password to access multiple university services, and UWA also uses MFA (multi-factor authentication) as a second form of identification for accessing systems. This is a strong digital security concept because it protects online services in the same way that a Campus Card protects physical spaces such as Reid Library: both are methods of verifying that the person requesting access is actually authorised. UWA also promotes the SafeZone app, which connects users directly with UWA Security, showing that campus security includes both physical safety and technology-based protection. Overall, the security concepts observed on campus show a layered approach: card access protects buildings, patrols and monitoring support physical safety, and UniID plus MFA protect digital systems and sensitive student information.
