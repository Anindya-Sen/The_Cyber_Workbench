# A07 – Discover cryptography used in modern networks


I am a big fan of online multiplayer games, and sometimes I want to play with people in a different location. That is how I came across VPNs, or Virtual Private Networks, and started looking into how they actually work. At first, I only knew that people used VPNs to connect through another location, but after learning more, I found out that a VPN is also a strong example of cryptography used in modern networks. Mozilla explain that a VPN creates an encrypted connection, often described as a secure tunnel, between the device and the VPN server. This means the internet traffic is protected before it continues across the wider network, instead of moving in a directly exposed form through the local connection. That made VPNs a strong example for this activity, because they show how modern networks use cryptography not only for access, but also for protecting communication while it is travelling.

In simple terms, a VPN works by taking the traffic from the device and scrambling it before it leaves, so that it cannot be easily understood while passing through the network. The data then travels through the VPN tunnel to the VPN server, where it is processed and sent on to the website or online service being used. Mozilla explains this as creating a private pathway between the device and the VPN provider. What makes this useful is that the traffic is hidden from the surrounding network while it is in transit, which adds a stronger layer of protection when using shared or unfamiliar connections. This makes VPNs a very practical example of cryptography in modern networks, because they show how encryption can be used to protect the flow of communication itself, not just the final website or app being accessed.
